# Research Report: How University Students Search for Course Information

**Date:** 2025-11-13
**Researcher:** Strategic Business Analyst Mary
**Participant:** BIP (Student at the university)
**Research Type:** User Research
**Focus:** How students currently search for course information for the Himolde Study Friend chatbot project.

---

## Executive Summary: Student Course Information Search Behavior

**Headline:** Students navigate a fragmented landscape of insufficient tools to find course information, leading to frustration and inefficiency.

**Critical Insights:**
1.  **Tool Proliferation & Inconsistency:** Students utilize various platforms (Canvas, university website, word of mouth, physical lectures), but these tools often lack consistent information, leading to a fragmented experience.
2.  **Navigation & Information Gaps:** Existing tools can be difficult to navigate, frequently lack comprehensive or up-to-date information, and are not updated regularly enough.
3.  **User Frustration & Inefficiency:** The current search process is time-consuming and frustrating, as students struggle to locate basic, essential course details.

**Recommended Path Forward for Himolde Study Friend Chatbot:**
1.  **Knowledge Base Centralization:** Prioritize the comprehensive gathering and structuring of accurate, up-to-date course information into a unified knowledge base.
2.  **Enhanced UI/UX:** Focus on designing an intuitive and user-friendly interface for the chatbot to ensure a seamless and efficient user experience.

**Key Challenges for the Chatbot to Address:**
1.  **Ambiguous User Intent:** Students often don't precisely know what information they need or how to articulate their queries effectively.
2.  **Information Discovery:** Students struggle with where to find specific information and what details are crucial for their academic success.
3.  **Time Sensitivity:** Students frequently operate under time pressure, requiring quick and accurate access to information.

---

## Research Findings: Current Search Methods

### Web Search Findings (Common Methods)
Based on a web search for "how university students search for course information canvas university website", common methods include:
-   **Canvas Dashboard and Courses List:** Students often start from their Canvas Dashboard or the 'All Courses' list to see current, past, and future enrollments.
-   **Search Functionality:** Both global search (across all courses) and course-specific search (within a single course for syllabus, assignments, etc.) are used. Search results can often be filtered.
-   **Accessing Past Courses:** Students can typically access content from previous semesters via 'Past Enrollments' on the 'All Courses' page.
-   **Mobile Applications:** Dedicated Canvas mobile apps are used for on-the-go access.
-   **Direct Instructor Guidance:** Instructors may provide specific links or instructions.

### First-Hand Observations (from Participant BIP)
The web search findings align with the participant's first-hand experience. Additionally, the following methods were observed:
-   **Word of Mouth:** Students often rely on peers for information.
-   **Physical Lectures:** Information is sometimes conveyed or clarified during in-person lectures.

## Conclusion

The research confirms a significant need for a centralized and efficient solution to course information access. The Himolde Study Friend chatbot has the potential to address critical pain points, but careful consideration of data accuracy, user experience, and the inherent challenges of student search behavior will be crucial for its success.
